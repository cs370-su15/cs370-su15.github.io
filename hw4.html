Teaching to a Group

Imagine you are a TA. Your discussion section have students of varying levels of understanding of the course material. Let's say your roster follows a normal distribution. So there are sizable minorities on the ends (some people struggling and some people really far ahead), with the majority of the people falling in the middle. Who do you teach to?

Vote and then post a followup on Piazza with your reasoning. This can also be a followup to another student's response either agreeing/disputing/commenting/etc their response. Make two such posts. I've made a dummy followup for the first few students since there won't be any followups to comment on at the beginning. It is my goal to have a lively and productive discussion about this on Piazza. I will reveal what I actually think in section this week.

Tutoring Journal

This week you should be tutoring at least two people (ideally three). In your bDrive folder, make a new document with the name of each student you tutored and write a summary of how it went. Think about the following questions while writing your summary:

  • What did you cover? Do you think the student was following? How do you think you did?
  • What techniques did you use that we have talked about in 370?
  • Did you use a whiteboard/paper? How long did it take you to get the marker/pen in the student's hand?
  • Is there anything you feel like you could have done better?
