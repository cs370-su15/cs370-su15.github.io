Tacit Vocabulary and Double Meanings

In class, we briefly covered the idea that teaching can be difficult if there are words that you use without explaining what they mean. Worse, there are words that have meaning outside of Computer Science, which confuse the student that you're working with because they don't know that they don't know the CS definition.

Your job: find one such word that might be confusing to students in 61A, and explain the outside contexnt and also the CS context in followup to this post. Don't post duplicates.

Here are examples (deliberately picked outside of 61A):

  • Positive Feedback - in control theory (introduced in EE20), means that a system is growing out of control (think microphone feedback, or the vicious cycle described in section). Whereas "positive feedback" usually means that you are being complimented in some way.
  • Induction - in CS theory (introduced in CS 70), Induction is a mathematical proof technique where you prove something recursively. In Physics, it has to do with forces created via magnets.
